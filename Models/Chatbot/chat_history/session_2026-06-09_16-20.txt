Why do tomato leaves turn yellow after transplanting?

✅ الإجابة:
**Cause:** The yellowing is caused by a conidial fungal infection (likely *Fusarium* or *Alternaria*), exacerbated by high fertilizer rates and water droplet spread. Transplanting stress weakens plants, making them susceptible.  

**Symptoms:** Lower leaves yellow, wilt severely during the day, and recover at night. Discoloration occurs >1 meter above soil, unlike root rot (which affects <15 cm). Reduced fruit yield.  

**Treatment:** Apply systemic fungicides (e.g., *azoxystrobin*, *propiconazole*). Remove and destroy infected plants to prevent spread. Reduce fertilizer application immediately.  

**Prevention:** Avoid over-fertilizing. Use drip irrigation to minimize water droplets. Rotate crops and ensure proper drainage. Monitor for early symptoms and act promptly.

❓ السؤال:
How can I manage high salinity in irrigation water?

✅ الإجابة:
**Cause:** High salinity in irrigation water (due to poor water quality, over-irrigation, or mineral accumulation) leads to osmotic stress, ion toxicity, and nutrient imbalance in plants.  

**Symptoms:** Yellowing leaves, stunted growth, reduced yields, leaf burn, and root damage. Soil may show crusting, reduced permeability, and salt crusts on the surface.  

**Treatment:**  
1. **Test water salinity** (EC/TDS) and adjust irrigation schedules to avoid overwatering.  
2. **Flush salts** from soil using excess water (leaching) during dry periods.  
3. **Apply gypsum (CaSO₄)** to displace sodium (Na⁺) and improve soil structure.  
4. **Use salt-tolerant crop varieties** (e.g., wheat strains resistant to Na⁺ toxicity).  
5. **Drain excess water** to prevent waterlogging and salt buildup.  

**Prevention:**  
1. **Monitor salinity regularly** using EC sensors and adjust water sources (e.g., switch to low-salinity water).  
2. **Implement drip irrigation** to minimize water use and salt accumulation.  
3. **Balance soil nutrients** with calcium (Ca²⁺) to counteract sodium (Na⁺) effects.  
4. **Avoid over-fertilization** to prevent salt buildup in soil.  
5. **Rotate crops** with salt-tolerant species to maintain soil health.

What is the best fertilization program for potatoes during the vegetative stage?

✅ الإجابة:
**Cause:** Deficiency of nitrogen (N) and potassium (K) during the vegetative stage of potato growth (leaf development) leads to stunted growth, chlorosis, and reduced tuber yield.  
**Symptoms:** Yellowing of leaves (N deficiency), weak stems, and poor tuber initiation.  
**Treatment:** Apply **0.3–0.5% urea (N)** + **0.2% potassium sulfate (K)** every 10–14 days during the vegetative stage. Adjust based on soil test results.  
**Prevention:** Conduct soil testing for N, P, and K levels; apply balanced NPK fertilizers (e.g., 10-20-20) at planting, and monitor soil pH (6.0–6.5) to optimize nutrient uptake. Avoid over-application of N to prevent leaching (→ runoff → R) and nutrient imbalance (→ N → S, D, AM, Fe, B).

✅ الإجابة:

**Symptoms**: Interveinal chlorosis on young leaves (veins remain green), leaf yellowing, stunted growth, and reduced fruit yield.  
**Treatment**: Apply chelated iron (e.g., Fe-EDDHA) via foliar sprays or soil incorporation. Adjust soil pH to 5.5–6.0 using sulfur. Avoid excess phosphorus.  
**Prevention**: Regular soil testing, maintain pH 5.5–6.0, use balanced fertilization, and ensure proper drainage to prevent root stress.

- Adopt AWD to optimize water use and root health.  
- Select lodging-resistant rice varieties.  
- Balance fertilization (N, P, K) based on soil tests.  
- Monitor weather patterns and avoid heavy irrigation during critical growth stages.

**Prevention:** Regular soil testing (every 2–3 years) and matching fertilizer rates to crop needs based on soil type and climate.
